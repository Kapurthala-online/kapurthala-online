Heritage Sites

Famous Monuments & Landmarks

🏛️
Royal Palace · 1908

Jagatjit Palace

Modelled on the Palace of Versailles by Maharaja Jagatjit Singh. Set in 44 acres of gardens, it now houses the prestigious Sainik School Kapurthala. Its baroque twin towers are the crown of the city's skyline.

🕌
Mosque · 1930

Moorish Mosque

Inspired by the architecture of Marrakesh, this stunning mosque was built in 1930 for Kapurthala's Muslim community. Its Moorish-style arches, domes, and minarets stand as a symbol of royal patronage and religious harmony.

🏰
Palace · Early 20th C.

Elysee Palace

Named after the Élysée Palace in Paris, this elegant structure blends European architectural grandeur with Indian sensibilities — another testament to the Maharaja's cosmopolitan vision.

🛕
Temple Complex

Panch Mandir

A revered temple complex comprising five shrines dedicated to different Hindu deities. A spiritual hub drawing thousands of devotees, especially during festivals like Navratri, Diwali, and Holi.

⛪
Sikh Shrine

Gurudwara Ber Sahib

Associated with Guru Nanak Dev Ji, this revered shrine is one of the most peaceful spots near Kapurthala. Legend holds that the Guru rested under a jujube (ber) tree here. Langar is served to all visitors daily.

🗼
Museum

Kapurthala State Museum

A curated collection of royal artefacts, weapons, period paintings, and historical documents from the Kapurthala State era. An immersive journey through the city's royal past and the Ahluwalia dynasty legacy.

Timeline

Kapurthala Through the Ages

~11th Century
Kapurthala founded — named after Kapur, a local chieftain of the Bhatti clan.
1772
Jassa Singh Ahluwalia establishes Kapurthala as a Sikh princely state.
1872
Maharaja Jagatjit Singh is born — the ruler who would transform Kapurthala into a city of palaces.
1900s
Construction of Jagatjit Palace begins. French architects are brought to design the city's grandest buildings.
1930
Moorish Mosque completed. Kapurthala gains international recognition for its fusion of Indian and European architecture.
1947
Kapurthala State merges with the Indian Union following Independence.
Today
Kapurthala thrives as a heritage tourism destination, sporting city, and home to a growing local economy — supported by Kapurthala Online.

💡 Did You Know?

Kapurthala was one of the most progressive princely states in colonial India — with electricity and running water before most cities.

Maharaja Jagatjit Singh spoke fluent French and was personally acquainted with French President Sadi Carnot.

The Sainik School Kapurthala, housed inside Jagatjit Palace, is one of India's most prestigious military schools.

Kapurthala has produced numerous national-level athletes, especially in field hockey and athletics.

Local Culture

Food, Festivals & Traditions

🍛

Punjabi Cuisine

Makki di Roti & Sarson da Saag, Amritsari Kulcha, Lassi, Pinni, and Gur Wali Kheer — Kapurthala's food culture is rich, hearty, and famous across Punjab.

🎉

Festivals

Baisakhi, Lohri, Diwali, Eid, Gurpurab — Kapurthala celebrates every festival with equal enthusiasm, reflecting its multi-religious and multicultural heritage.

🎭

Folk Arts

Bhangra, Gidda, traditional Phulkari embroidery, and Punjabi folk music are deeply woven into everyday life and public celebrations.

🏑

Sports

Kapurthala has a proud sporting culture with Field Hockey, Kabaddi, and Athletics. It hosts several state-level tournaments each year and sends athletes to national competitions.
